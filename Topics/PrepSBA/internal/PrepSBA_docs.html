    1. Product Overview

    1.1 What is PrepSBA?

    PrepSBA is a guided web application that helps small business owners prepare smarter and faster for SBA loans. The product walks borrowers through a structured intake workflow, then uses AI to generate a complete lender-ready SBA loan package.

    The positioning is simple: TurboTax for SBA loan applications and business plans. Instead of forcing a borrower to manually assemble scattered forms, spreadsheets, narratives, projections, and supporting explanations, PrepSBA turns the process into a guided wizard with contextual help and export-ready deliverables.

    1.2 Who It’s For

    • Small business owners seeking SBA 7(a), SBA Express, or similar lender-backed financing.
    • First-time borrowers who need clear explanations of what lenders ask for and why.
    • Entrepreneurs buying, starting, or expanding a business who need business plan and financial package support.
    • Lenders and lending partners who may later use a branded portal to improve borrower intake quality and reduce incomplete submissions.

    1.3 Core Value Proposition

    • Prepare faster: one guided workflow instead of disconnected templates and forms.
    • Improve completeness: real-time validation and step-by-step requirements reduce missing data.
    • Generate lender-ready materials: business plan, financial projections, SBA form data, use-of-proceeds, debt schedule, and cover materials.
    • Reduce intimidation: TurboTax-style explanations, tooltips, and “why we need this” guidance.
    • Monetize at export: free preparation lowers adoption friction; paid export captures value when the borrower is ready.

    2. User Flow and Product Experience

    2.1 Primary Borrower Flow

    Landing Page → Sign Up / Log In → Dashboard → New Application
  → Step 1: Business Identity
  → Step 2: Ownership & Borrower Info
  → Step 3: Loan Request & Use of Proceeds
  → Step 4: Financial Snapshot
  → Step 5: Market & Competition
  → Step 6: Management & Experience
  → Step 7: Review & Generate
    → AI generates documents
    → User reviews, edits, exports PDF / DOCX

    2.2 Wizard Principles

    • Auto-save: each step saves incrementally to avoid lost work.
    • Progress tracking: application state and current step are persistent.
    • Inline validation: required fields and obvious errors are caught at entry time.
    • Contextual help: explain terms like NAICS, use of proceeds, collateral, DSCR, working capital, guarantor, and ownership percentage.
    • Review before generation: user sees a consolidated summary before paying/exporting.

    2.3 Generated Deliverables

    • Full business plan narrative
    • Pro forma income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow
    • 3–5 year cash flow projections
    • Competitor analysis
    • Borrower experience / management resume narrative
    • Personal financial statement data for SBA Form 413
    • SBA Form 1919 borrower information data
    • Business debt schedule
    • Use-of-proceeds breakdown
    • Loan summary and cover letter

    4. Architecture

    4.1 System Overview

    Browser UI
  ↓
Next.js App Router pages + components
  ↓
Server actions / API routes
  ├── Supabase Auth
  ├── Supabase PostgreSQL + RLS
  ├── Stripe checkout / billing webhooks
  ├── OpenAI document generation
  └── PDF/DOCX export services

    4.2 Frontend Architecture

    • Landing and marketing pages: optimized for conversion, SEO, and clear product education.
    • Auth pages: login/signup with brand-aligned mint/navy design.
    • Dashboard: shows applications, status, next step, and create-new flow.
    • Application wizard: route-per-step or shared step component under /apply/[id]/step-n.
    • Review/generate: consolidated summary, validation, payment/export gating.

    4.3 Backend Architecture

    • Application CRUD: create drafts, update steps, track completion.
    • Validation: schema validation with Zod or equivalent before database writes and AI generation.
    • AI generation service: transforms normalized application data into controlled prompts and structured outputs.
    • Export service: converts generated structured content into DOCX/PDF packages.
    • Billing service: controls export access after Stripe payment confirmation.

    4.4 Data Security Architecture

    • Supabase Row Level Security ensures users can access only their own applications.
    • Sensitive borrower PII should never be written into logs.
    • SSN or equivalent high-sensitivity fields should be encrypted server-side if stored.
    • Document generations and exports should be audit logged.
    • Users must be able to delete applications and associated generated files.

    6. AI and Document Generation

    6.1 AI Principles

    • Structured inputs first: AI should generate from normalized application data, not raw chat only.
    • Controlled templates: prompts should be versioned and tied to expected output schemas.
    • User review required: AI outputs are draft/preparation materials, not legal/financial advice.
    • Traceability: store prompt version, model, timestamp, and source data snapshot hash for each generation.

    6.2 Generation Pipeline

    Validate application completeness
  → Build structured data package
  → Run document-specific prompts
  → Parse structured JSON output
  → Render preview in app
  → Allow edits/revisions
  → Export DOCX/PDF after payment

    6.3 Prompt Families

    • Business plan narrative prompt
    • Financial projection explanation prompt
    • Competitor and market analysis prompt
    • Management/resume narrative prompt
    • Loan cover letter prompt
    • Use-of-proceeds and loan summary prompt
    • SBA form data formatting prompt

    8. Marketing Strategy

    8.1 Target Segments

    • Borrowers: small business owners, franchise buyers, acquisition entrepreneurs, contractors, local service businesses, startups with SBA-compatible plans.
    • Referral partners: accountants, bookkeepers, SBA consultants, business brokers, franchise consultants, local chambers, SCORE-style advisors.
    • Lenders: community banks, credit unions, SBA-preferred lenders, loan brokers, alternative finance advisors.

    8.2 Acquisition Channels

    • SEO content around SBA loan documents, business plan templates, SBA Form 413, SBA Form 1919, use of proceeds, debt schedules, and SBA loan preparation checklists.
    • Forum and community marketing in small business, acquisition entrepreneurship, franchise, and startup finance communities.
    • Email outreach to accountants, brokers, consultants, chambers, and lending-adjacent partners.
    • Affiliate/referral program for partners who send borrower leads.
    • Future lender channel as B2B expansion after borrower workflow proves demand.

    8.3 SEO Content Plan

    • “SBA loan application checklist”
    • “How to prepare an SBA business plan”
    • “SBA Form 413 explained”
    • “SBA Form 1919 explained”
    • “Use of proceeds for SBA loan examples”
    • “Business debt schedule template”
    • “SBA loan financial projections template”

    8.4 Conversion Strategy

    • Lead with free guided preparation to reduce friction.
    • Gate final export behind one-time payment after the borrower has invested effort and sees value.
    • Show sample outputs before signup where possible.
    • Use trust markers: privacy, secure storage, no lender submission without user approval, clear disclaimers.

    8.5 Marketing/Legal Disclaimers

    PrepSBA should avoid claiming guaranteed approval, guaranteed funding, official SBA affiliation, or legal/financial advice. Suggested language: “PrepSBA helps you prepare application materials. It does not guarantee loan approval and is not affiliated with or endorsed by the U.S. Small Business Administration.”

    9. Security, Privacy, and Compliance

    • TLS for all traffic.
    • Supabase encryption at rest.
    • RLS policies for every borrower-owned table.
    • No sensitive PII in analytics, logs, prompt logs, or browser console output.
    • Clear privacy policy covering AI processing and third-party providers.
    • Export audit log for paid packages.
    • Data deletion workflow for borrower applications and generated documents.
    • Future lender portal should add team roles, audit trails, and stricter access controls.
